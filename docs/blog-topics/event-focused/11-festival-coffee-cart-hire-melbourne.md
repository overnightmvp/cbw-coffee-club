### Pricing Models for Festivals (500 words)

**1. Flat Fee Model**
- Vendor pays organizer $X for pitch
- Vendor keeps 100% of coffee sales
- Typical: $300-800/day depending on festival size
- Risk: Low foot traffic = vendor loss

**2. Revenue Share Model**
- Vendor pays % of coffee sales to organizer
- Typical: 10-20% of gross sales
- Or: Minimum guarantee + revenue share (e.g., $200 + 10%)
- Preferred by large festivals

**3. Organizer-Paid Model** (rare)
- Organizer pays vendor flat fee for service
- Coffee free to festival-goers (VIP areas)
- Or: Subsidized pricing
- Typical: Corporate festivals, brand activations

**Which Model is Best?**
- Small festivals (<1000 people): Flat fee
- Large festivals (5000+ people): Revenue share
- Corporate/branded events: Organizer-paid

### Capacity Planning for Festivals (400 words)

**Guest Count Challenges**
- Unpredictable attendance
- Weather-dependent turnout
- Staggered arrival (not everyone at once)

**Coffee Demand Multipliers**
- Morning: 30-40% of attendees order
- Midday: 15-20% order
- Afternoon: 10-15% order
- All-day festival: 0.3-0.4 cups per attendee (spread over time)

**Barista Staffing**
- Small market (500 people): 1 barista
- Medium festival (2000 people): 2 baristas or 2 carts
- Large festival (5000+ people): 3-4 baristas across multiple carts
- Peak hour capacity: Plan for queue management

**Multiple Cart Strategy**
- Spread carts across venue (reduce congestion)
- Different offerings (espresso vs cold brew vs specialty)
- Brand activation (multiple sponsors = multiple carts)

### Festival-Specific Setup Requirements (500 words)

**Power Challenges**
- Generators usually required (venues lack outlets)
- Fuel capacity for all-day service (20L+ tank)
- Noise regulations (generator decibel limits)
- Generator rental: $150-300/day

**Space & Location**
- High-foot-traffic areas (near main stage, entry/exit)
- Avoid dead zones
- Minimum 3m x 3m (includes marquee)
- Queue space: 4-5m buffer

**Weather Protection**
- Marquee essential (rain or shine)
- Wind stakes/weights required
- Backup plan for severe weather

**Logistics**
- Early load-in (5-6am typical)
- Vehicle access (before crowd arrival)
- Water refill access (90L tanks run out)
- Waste management (grounds, wastewater)
- Late pack-down (after crowd leaves)

### Festival Permit Requirements (300 words)

**Vendor Must Have:**
- Food business registration
- Mobile food vendor permit (council-specific)
- Public liability insurance ($10M-$20M for large events)
- WorkSafe compliance (employee safety)

**Organizer Responsibilities:**
- Event permit (>500 people typically)
- Vendor coordination and scheduling
- Site plan submission to council
- Waste management plan

**Council-Specific:**
- City of Melbourne: Strict regulations
- Regional councils: More flexible
- Park/beach permits: Department of Environment

### Menu Optimization for Festivals (400 words)

**Speed is Key**
- Simpler menu = faster service = more sales
- Focus on high-volume drinks (lattes, cappuccinos, long blacks)
- Avoid slow specialty drinks (unless premium festival)

**Pricing Strategy**
- Festival premium: $1-2 above street prices
- Typical: $5-7 for latte (vs $4.50-5.50 at cafes)
- Convenience pricing

**Alternative Milk**
- Essential: Oat, almond, soy
- 30-40% of festival-goers request alternatives

**Cold Brew & Iced Drinks**
- Summer festivals: 50% of orders may be iced
- Pre-batch cold brew for speed
- Equipment: Cold brew kegs, ice supply

**Non-Coffee Options**
- Chai, hot chocolate, tea
- Kids' drinks (babycino, hot chocolate)
- Bottled water (easy addon sales)

### Case Studies: Melbourne Festivals (400 words)

**Case Study: St Kilda Festival**
- Attendance: 40,000+ over 1 day
- Coffee carts: 8-10 vendors
- Typical sales per cart: 1000-1500 cups
- Revenue: $6000-9000 per cart
- Challenge: Peak hour queues, weather (summer)

**Case Study: Moomba Festival**
- Attendance: 1M+ over 4 days
- CBD location, high foot traffic
- Premium pricing ($7-8 lattes)
- Multiple cart locations across Alexandra Gardens

**Case Study: Night Noodle Markets**
- Evening festival (5pm-11pm)
- Lower coffee demand (20% of noodle sales)
- Specialty drinks (iced, cold brew) perform well
- Longer service window (6 hours nightly)

### Vendor Selection for Festivals (300 words)

**What Festival Organizers Look For:**
- Experience with high-volume service
- Generator and marquee ownership (self-sufficient)
- Reliability (show up on time, stay full duration)
- Professional presentation (clean cart, branded)
- Positive reviews from other festivals

**What Vendors Look For:**
- Guaranteed foot traffic (attendance projections)
- Fair pitch fees or revenue share
- Early load-in access
- Power/water access (if available)
- Marketing support (festival promotion)

### Questions for Festival Vendors (250 words)

**For Organizers Hiring Vendors:**
1. Have you worked festivals before? (experience)
2. Do you have your own generator and marquee? (self-sufficiency)
3. What's your capacity per hour? (queue management)
4. Do you have insurance ($20M for large events)?
5. What's your menu and pricing?
6. Can you handle all-day service?

**For Vendors Considering Festivals:**
1. Expected attendance? (revenue projection)
2. Pitch fee or revenue share? (cost structure)
3. Power and water access? (equipment needs)
4. Load-in and pack-down times? (logistics)
5. How many other coffee vendors? (competition)
6. Marketing support? (foot traffic drivers)
