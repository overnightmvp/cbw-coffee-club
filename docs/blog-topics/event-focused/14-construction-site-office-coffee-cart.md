### Construction Site Coffee Carts (600 words)

**Why Construction Sites Need Coffee Carts**
- Remote locations (no nearby cafes)
- Early start times (6am-7am)
- Large crews (50-200 workers)
- Safety concern (workers leaving site for coffee)
- Morale booster (tough physical work)

**Service Model**
- **Morning service**: 6:30am-8:30am (pre-shift + first break)
- **Duration**: 1.5-2 hours typically
- **Frequency**: Daily (Mon-Fri) or weekly (same day each week)
- **Pricing**: $400-700/visit, volume discounts for recurring

**Setup Requirements**
- **Power**: Generator required (most sites lack outlets)
- **Space**: Flat area near site office/amenities
- **Access**: Vehicle access for delivery (mud, gravel considerations)
- **Weather protection**: Marquee for rain (workers queue outside)
- **Safety**: Hi-vis vest for barista, site induction

**Menu for Construction Workers**
- Simple, fast menu (long black, latte, cappuccino, flat white)
- Large sizes (extra shot = extra energy)
- Value pricing ($4.50-5.50 for loyalty)
- Bulk order discount (foreman orders for crew)
- Sausage rolls / pastries (upsell opportunity)

**Capacity for Large Crews**
- 100 workers, 70% uptake = 70 cups in 1.5 hours
- Need: 1-2 baristas depending on speed
- Pre-orders help (foreman submits crew orders ahead)

**Case Study: Major Melbourne Build Site**
- Location: CBD high-rise construction
- Crew: 150 workers
- Service: Daily 6:30am-8am (Mon-Fri)
- Setup: 2 baristas, dual-station cart
- Average daily cups: 110
- Monthly contract: $8000 (20 working days @ $400/day)
- Worker satisfaction: High (reduced off-site coffee runs)

**Benefits to Construction Companies**
- **Safety**: Workers stay on-site (no traffic risks)
- **Productivity**: Less downtime (5-min queue vs 20-min cafe trip)
- **Morale**: Perceived perk (employee satisfaction)
- **Cost-effective**: Compared to individual coffees ($5.50 vs $4.50 bulk)

**Challenges**
- Weather (rain, extreme cold/heat)
- Site access (security, parking)
- Early morning timing (limited vendor availability)
- Long-term contracts (3-6 month build duration)

### Corporate Office Coffee Service (700 words)

**Why Offices Need Coffee Carts**
- Alternative to office coffee machines (better quality)
- Employee perk (attract/retain talent)
- Morning productivity boost
- Networking/culture building (water cooler replacement)

**Service Models**

**Model 1: Weekly Popup (Most Common)**
- **Frequency**: Once per week (e.g., "Free Coffee Fridays")
- **Duration**: 1-2 hours (8am-10am typical)
- **Pricing**: $500-800/week
- **Best for**: 50-150 person offices

**Model 2: Daily Morning Service**
- **Frequency**: Mon-Fri, 8am-10am
- **Duration**: 2 hours daily
- **Pricing**: $1800-2500/week (volume discount)
- **Best for**: 200+ person offices, high-growth tech companies

**Model 3: Full-Day Barista Station**
- **Frequency**: Mon-Fri, 8am-5pm
- **Duration**: 9 hours daily
- **Pricing**: $4000-6000/week (dedicated barista)
- **Best for**: Large corporate HQs (500+ employees)

**Model 4: Special Occasion Service**
- Company town halls, quarterly meetings, team celebrations
- One-off service (not recurring)
- **Pricing**: $600-1000/event

**Office Setup Requirements**
- **Breakroom/kitchen**: Most common location
- **Lobby**: High-visibility option
- **Outdoor terrace**: Summer option (weather-dependent)
- **Power**: Standard office outlets (2x 10-amp)
- **Space**: 2m x 2m, near seating area
- **Access**: Elevator capacity, load-in timing (before 8am)

**Employer-Paid vs Employee-Paid**

**Free Coffee (Employer Pays)**
- **Pros**: Maximum employee benefit, culture booster, simple
- **Cons**: Higher cost ($500-800/week)
- **Best for**: Tech startups, high-margin businesses, recruitment tool

**Subsidized Coffee (Employer + Employee Split)**
- **Model**: Employer pays $300/week, employees pay $3/coffee (vs $5.50 market rate)
- **Pros**: Shared cost, employees still save money
- **Cons**: Requires payment system (card reader, app)

**Employee-Paid (Discounted)**
- **Model**: Vendor offers employee discount (e.g., $4.50 vs $5.50)
- **Pros**: No employer cost, still a perk (convenience + discount)
- **Cons**: Lower uptake (employees may go to nearby cafe instead)

**ROI for Employers**
- **Cost**: $500/week for 75-person office = $6.67/employee/week
- **Benefit**: Reduced coffee runs (15 min saved/day = 1.25 hours/week)
- **Productivity value**: $6.67 for 1.25 hours = ~$5/hour (huge ROI)
- **Retention**: Perceived as valuable perk (low-cost employee satisfaction)

**Popular Office Coffee Days**
- **Monday mornings**: "Kick off the week" energy
- **Wednesday**: "Hump day" morale boost
- **Friday**: "Thank God It's Friday" culture (most popular)

**Case Study: Melbourne Tech Startup**
- Employees: 80 people
- Service: Every Friday, 8:30am-10:30am (2 hours)
- Setup: Breakroom, free for employees
- Cost: $650/week ($33,800/year)
- Uptake: 65 employees average (81%)
- Employee feedback: #1 rated office perk
- Retention impact: Cited in exit interviews as valued benefit

**Long-Term Contracts**

### Booking Recurring Services (300 words)

**Trial Period Recommended**
- Book 4-week trial before committing long-term
- Test uptake, gather employee feedback
- Adjust timing/menu based on trial
- Then commit to 6-12 month contract

**Scheduling Flexibility**
- Skip weeks for public holidays
- Adjust timing for team events
- Add extra days for special occasions
- Cancel with 48-hour notice (contract-dependent)

**Vendor Reliability Essential**
- Recurring service = consistency matters
- Same barista team (employees build rapport)
- Backup plan for barista sick days
- Communication for any changes

**Payment Terms**
- Monthly invoicing (vs per-visit)
- Corporate credit card or invoice payment
- Net 30 terms typical for recurring contracts
