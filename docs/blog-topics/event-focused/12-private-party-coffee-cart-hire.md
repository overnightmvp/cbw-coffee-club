### Private Party Pricing (400 words)

**Typical Cost Range**
- Small party (20-40 guests): $450-600 for 2 hours
- Medium party (40-80 guests): $600-800 for 2-3 hours
- Large private party (80-150 guests): $800-1200 for 3 hours

**What's Included (Standard):**
- 1 barista for 2-3 hours
- All coffee equipment (espresso machine, grinder, cart)
- Premium coffee beans
- Milk options (dairy, oat, almond, soy)
- Cups, lids, stirrers, napkins
- Setup and pack-down

**Add-Ons & Extras:**
- Second barista: +$150-200
- Extended hours: +$100-150/hour
- Specialty drinks (cold brew, flavored syrups): +$50-100
- Custom signage (birthday message, monogram): +$50-150
- Dessert pairing (cookies, cakes): Vendor-dependent

**Budget-Friendly Tips:**
- Book off-peak days (weekdays, Sundays)
- Morning or afternoon (avoid dinner time premiums)
- Shorter service window (1.5 hours vs 3 hours)
- Smaller guest count (under 50)

### Timing Your Coffee Service (400 words)

**Birthday Parties**

*Morning/Brunch Parties (9am-12pm)*
- Perfect for coffee cart
- Guests arrive caffeinated and energized
- Pair with breakfast foods
- Service time: 2-3 hours

*Afternoon Parties (2pm-5pm)*
- Post-lunch lull = perfect for coffee
- Pairs with cake and desserts
- Service time: 1.5-2 hours

*Evening Parties (7pm-late)*
- Less demand for coffee (cocktails more popular)
- Consider as "second wind" at 9pm (after dinner)
- Or Irish coffee / coffee cocktails
- Service time: 1-1.5 hours

**Anniversaries**

*Morning-After Brunch*
- Classic choice for overnight guests
- Casual, relaxed vibe
- Service time: 2-3 hours (guests arrive staggered)

*Afternoon Celebration*
- Garden party with coffee + tea
- Pair with light finger foods
- Service time: 2 hours

**Baby Showers & Gender Reveals**
- Usually daytime (11am-3pm)
- Many pregnant guests = decaf essential
- Service time: 2 hours
- Coordinate with food service timing

### Home Party Logistics (500 words)

**Space Requirements**
- Backyard: Ideal (2m x 2m flat area)
- Garage: Works well (open door for ventilation)
- Driveway: Possible (with marquee)
- Indoor (living room, sunroom): Check doorway width (90cm min)

**Power Access**
- Need 2x standard outlets within 10m of cart location
- Extension cords: Vendor provides
- Avoid: Sharing circuit with high-draw appliances (AC, heater)
- Test outlets beforehand (don't want tripped breaker mid-party)

**Weather Considerations (Outdoor)**
- Marquee highly recommended (rain or shine)
- Wind: Secure setup if >20km/h forecast
- Hot weather: Shade essential (direct sun affects machine)
- Cold weather: Machine takes longer to warm up (communicate timing)

**Guest Flow**
- Position cart near main gathering area
- Not blocking foot traffic
- Near seating (guests can sit with coffee)
- Away from food service area (separate queues)

**Parking & Access**
- Vendor needs parking within 50m (load equipment)
- Driveway access ideal
- Narrow side gates: May not work (check cart dimensions)
- Stairs: Most carts cannot navigate

**Setup Timing**
- Vendor arrives 45-60 min before service
- Setup: 30-40 minutes
- Best: Before guests arrive (so cart is ready)
- Example: Guests arrive 2pm, vendor arrives 1:15pm

**Dietary Considerations**
- Vegan guests: Oat/almond milk

### Questions to Ask Vendors (250 words)

1. **Do you service residential areas?** (some vendors only do commercial)
2. **Minimum guest count?** (some require 30+ people)
3. **Can you accommodate home power?** (standard outlets vs industrial)
4. **Customization options?** (signage, cups, menu)
5. **Setup space requirements?** (exact dimensions)
6. **Indoor or outdoor?** (some vendors prefer one)
7. **Parking and access needs?** (driveway, street parking)
8. **Service timing flexibility?** (can we adjust start time day-of?)
9. **Decaf and alternative milk included?** (some charge extra)
10. **Kids' drinks available?** (babycino, hot chocolate)

### DIY Coffee Station vs Hiring a Cart (300 words)

**DIY Coffee Station**
- **Pros**: Cheaper ($50-100 for supplies), full control
- **Cons**: Instant/drip coffee only (not espresso), messy cleanup, host must manage, lower quality
- **Best for**: Very small gatherings (<15 people), budget under $400

**Hiring a Coffee Cart**
- **Pros**: Professional quality, hands-off for host, guest experience, espresso drinks
- **Cons**: $450-600+ investment
- **Best for**: 25+ guests, special occasions, when quality matters

**When to Choose Cart:**
- Milestone celebration (not just any birthday)
- You want to impress guests
- Budget allows $450+
- 25+ guests (cost per head becomes reasonable)
- Host wants to actually enjoy the party
