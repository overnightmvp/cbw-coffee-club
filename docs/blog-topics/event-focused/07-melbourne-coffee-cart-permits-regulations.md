### Vendor Licensing Requirements (400 words)

**Mandatory for All Coffee Cart Vendors**

**1. Food Business Registration**
- Register with local council
- Renewed annually
- Mobile food vendor classification
- Cost: $150-400/year (council-dependent)

**2. Food Safety Supervisor Certification**
- At least one staff member certified
- HACCP-based training
- Nationally recognized: Food Safety Supervisor Certificate

**3. Public Liability Insurance**
- Minimum $10M coverage (industry standard)
- Covers customer injury, property damage
- Event organizers should verify before booking

**4. Mobile Vendor Permit** (location-dependent)
- Required for public spaces (parks, streets, beaches)
- NOT required for private venues (function centers, corporate offices)
- Obtained from local council
- Cost: $50-300/event

### Private Venue Events (Most Common) (300 words)

**No Special Permits Typically Required**
- Function centers, hotels, private properties
- Venue manages own approvals
- Vendor needs: Standard licensing (above)
- Event organizer needs: Venue booking only

**Venue May Require**
- Proof of insurance certificate
- Food safety documentation

- Setup/breakdown plan

**TBR Vendor Verification**

### Public Space Events (Parks, Beaches, Streets) (500 words)

**Requires Additional Permits**

**1. Mobile Food Vendor Permit**
- Issued by City of Melbourne or relevant council
- Application lead time: 2-4 weeks
- Cost: $50-300 per event
- Covers: Street trading, park trading

**2. Event Permit** (if large gathering)
- Required for 200+ people (varies by council)
- Lead time: 6-8 weeks
- Covers: Public gathering, noise, waste
- Cost: $200-2000 (size-dependent)

**Melbourne Council-Specific**
- City of Melbourne: Most restrictive
- Port Phillip, Yarra, Stonnington: Moderate
- Outer suburbs: More flexible

**Application Process**
- Submit site plan
- Waste management plan
- Public liability insurance
- Risk assessment (large events)
- Food safety documentation

### Health & Safety Compliance (400 words)

**Food Standards Australia New Zealand (FSANZ)**
- Governs all food handling
- Temperature control for milk
- Hand washing facilities (vendor provides)
- Pest control measures

**Equipment Safety**
- Electrical safety testing (annual)
- Gas safety (if applicable)
- Fire extinguisher on cart
- First aid kit

**WorkSafe Victoria**
- Safe work practices
- Barista training/competency
- Hazard management

### Insurance Requirements (300 words)

**Public Liability Insurance**
- $10M coverage minimum
- $20M for large events (recommended)
- Covers: Customer injury, property damage, third-party claims

**Product Liability**
- Covers food-borne illness claims
- Typically bundled with public liability

**Workers Compensation**
- Required if vendor has employees
- Covers barista injury on-site

**Event Organizer Insurance**
- Consider event-specific policy
- May require proof of vendor insurance

### Alcohol Licensing (if applicable) (250 words)

**Coffee Cocktails / Irish Coffee**
- If serving alcohol-infused drinks
- Requires liquor license or extension
- Vendor must hold RSA (Responsible Service of Alcohol)
- Rare for coffee carts, but emerging trend

### Waste Management Regulations (200 words)

**Vendor Responsibility**
- Coffee grounds disposal plan
- Wastewater disposal (onboard tank)
- Recyclable cup options (council-dependent)

**Event Organizer**
- Bins provided near cart
- Waste removal plan
- Compost option (if available)

### Checklist for Event Organizers (300 words)

**Before Booking Vendor:**
- [ ] Verify vendor food business registration
- [ ] Request public liability insurance certificate
- [ ] Confirm food safety supervisor certification

**For Private Venue Events:**
- [ ] Confirm venue allows external catering
- [ ] Check venue insurance requirements
- [ ] Coordinate access with venue

**For Public Space Events:**
- [ ] Apply for mobile food vendor permit (2-4 weeks ahead)
- [ ] Apply for event permit if 200+ guests (6-8 weeks ahead)
- [ ] Submit site plan to council
- [ ] Arrange waste management
