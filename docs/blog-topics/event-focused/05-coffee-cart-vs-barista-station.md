## Outline

### Introduction (150 words)
- Event planners face this decision often
- Both deliver quality coffee, but different experiences
- This guide helps you choose based on your event needs

### What is a Coffee Cart? (250 words)
- Mobile, self-contained unit
- Vintage to modern aesthetic options
- Full espresso setup on wheels
- Typically 2m x 2m footprint
- Power requirements: 2x 10-amp
- Setup time: 30-40 minutes
- Visual appeal and branding opportunities

### What is a Barista Station? (250 words)
- Fixed setup using venue space/tables
- Professional espresso machine + portable setup
- More flexible space requirements
- Can integrate into existing venue layout
- Lower visual impact (less "event feature")
- Faster setup in some cases

### Head-to-Head Comparison (600 words)

**Aesthetic & Experience**
- Cart: Instagram-worthy, conversation piece, branded
- Station: Professional but less visual impact
- Winner: Cart for aesthetic-focused events

**Space & Setup**
- Cart: Needs specific 2m x 2m space, defined footprint
- Station: More flexible, can adapt to venue
- Winner: Station for tight or irregular spaces

**Pricing**
- Cart: $450-800 for 2 hours (Melbourne)
- Station: $400-700 for 2 hours (slightly less)
- Winner: Station (marginally cheaper)

**Capacity**
- Cart: 80 cups/hour per barista (standard)
- Station: 80 cups/hour per barista (same)
- Winner: Tie (depends on barista count, not format)

**Event Integration**
- Cart: Standalone feature, draws attention
- Station: Blends into venue, less intrusive
- Winner: Depends on event goals

**Indoor vs Outdoor**
- Cart: Excellent outdoors, good indoors
- Station: Better indoors, challenging outdoors
- Winner: Cart for versatility

### Best Use Cases for Coffee Carts (300 words)
- Outdoor events (festivals, markets, garden weddings)
- Events where coffee is a feature (coffee-themed, networking)
- Weddings (photo opportunities)
- Brand activations (custom branding)
- Large venues (cart becomes destination)
- Events prioritizing experience over function

### Best Use Cases for Barista Stations (300 words)
- Tight indoor spaces
- Budget-conscious events
- Multi-day conferences (permanent setup)
- Corporate offices (breakroom setup)
- Events where coffee is support, not feature
- Venues with existing counter space

### Hybrid Option: Coffee Truck (200 words)
- Larger setup than cart
- Self-powered (generator)
- Higher capacity
- Best for: Large festivals, construction sites
- Price premium

### Decision Framework (400 words)

**Choose a Coffee Cart if:**
- Guest experience is priority
- Outdoor or versatile venue
- Wedding or aesthetic-focused event
- Want photo-worthy feature
- Branding opportunities matter
- Budget allows ($50-100 more)

**Choose a Barista Station if:**
- Space is limited
- Budget is tight
- Indoor corporate event
- Coffee is functional need, not experience
- Multi-day setup
- Venue has existing counter space

**Questions to ask yourself:**
1. What's the event vibe? (Formal vs casual)
2. Is coffee a feature or functional need?
3. Indoor or outdoor?
4. What's the space like?
5. What's the budget?
6. How important are photos/aesthetics?

- Some venues dictate choice
- Consider guest experience goals
