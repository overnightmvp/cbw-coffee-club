### Why You Need a Written Contract (250 words)
- Clarifies expectations and deliverables
- Legal protection if something goes wrong
- Financial safeguards (deposits, cancellations)
- Peace of mind for both parties
- Professional vendors always provide contracts

**When to Insist on a Contract:**
- Events >$500 (always)
- Large events (100+ guests)
- Corporate/paid events (liability concerns)
- Long booking lead time (months ahead)

**When Casual Agreement May Work:**
- Small private events (<50 guests, <$400)
- Short booking window (<2 weeks)
- Trusted vendor (repeat business)
- *Still recommended to get terms in writing (email confirmation)*

### Essential Contract Elements (800 words)

**1. Parties & Event Details**
- Full vendor business name + ABN
- Event organizer name + contact
- Event date, start/end time
- Venue name and address
- Setup location (specific within venue)

**2. Services & Deliverables**
- Number of baristas
- Service duration (e.g., "2 hours of service")
- Setup and pack-down time
- Menu offerings (specific drinks included)
- Expected guest count
- Equipment provided (cart description, espresso machine, etc.)

**3. Pricing & Payment Terms**
- Total cost (ex-GST and inc-GST)
- Itemized breakdown (base rate, add-ons, travel)
- Deposit amount and due date (typical: 25-50%)
- Balance due date (often: 7 days before event)
- Payment methods accepted
- Late payment penalties (if any)
- GST details (vendor's ABN)

**4. Cancellation Policy**
- Notice periods for cancellation
- Refund schedule:
  - >4 weeks notice: Full refund minus deposit
  - 2-4 weeks: 50% refund
  - <2 weeks: No refund (typical)
- Weather cancellation policy (outdoor events)
- Force majeure clause (COVID, natural disasters)

**5. Rescheduling Terms**
- Ability to postpone (within X months)
- Rescheduling fees (if any)
- Deposit transfer policy

**6. Guest Count Changes**
- Final headcount deadline (usually 7 days before)
- Price adjustment for significant changes (e.g., Â±25%)
- Maximum capacity guaranteed

**7. Venue & Setup Requirements**
- Power requirements (vendor confirms availability)
- Space requirements
- Access times (load-in/load-out)
- Parking arrangements
- Venue restrictions acknowledged

**8. Insurance & Liability**
- Vendor public liability insurance (min $10M)
- Proof of insurance provided
- Liability for equipment damage
- Food safety compliance commitment
- Customer injury liability (vendor's responsibility)

**9. Vendor Responsibilities**
- Professional presentation
- Food safety compliance
- Equipment maintenance
- Waste disposal
- Cleanup of service area

**10. Client Responsibilities**
- Venue access coordination
- Power provision
- Accurate guest count
- Payment on schedule
- Venue compliance (if applicable)

### Fair vs Unfair Terms (500 words)

**Fair Cancellation Policy Example:**
- >30 days: Full refund minus 10% admin fee
- 15-30 days: 50% refund
- <15 days: Deposit forfeited, 50% of balance due
- <7 days: Full payment due
- Weather cancellation (outdoor): Reschedule or 75% refund

**Unfair Cancellation Policy Example:**
- Any cancellation: 100% of payment due
- No refunds under any circumstance
- No rescheduling option

**Fair Guest Count Adjustment:**
- Final count due 7 days before
- Â±10%: No price change
- >25% decrease: Price adjusted proportionally
- >25% increase: Requires second barista (additional cost)

**Unfair Guest Count Terms:**
- "No changes after booking" (even months ahead)
- Price increase for any decrease in guests

**Fair Payment Terms:**
- 25-50% deposit at booking
- Balance due 7 days before event
- Accepted methods: Bank transfer, credit card, PayPal

**Unfair Payment Terms:**
- 100% upfront (months ahead)
- Cash only (no paper trail)
- Balance due on event day (awkward)

### Questions to Ask Before Signing (300 words)

1. **What if I need to cancel?**
   - Refund policy for various timeframes?

2. **What if you (vendor) cancel?**
   - Full refund + compensation?

3. **What's included in the price?**
   - Hidden fees? Travel? Equipment? Cups?

4. **What happens if equipment fails?**
   - Backup plan? Refund? Partial service?

5. **Can I see proof of insurance?**
   - Request certificate of currency

6. **What if guest count changes significantly?**
   - Price adjustment terms?

7. **What if we run over time?**
   - Extra hour pricing? Flexibility?

8. **What's the setup/pack-down timeline?**
   - Included in service time or extra?

9. **What if there's bad weather?** (outdoor events)
   - Reschedule option? Refund?

10. **What payment methods do you accept?**
    - Deposit + balance payment process?

### Negotiable vs Non-Negotiable Terms (300 words)

**Usually Negotiable:**
- Payment schedule (deposit % and timing)
- Cancellation notice periods
- Rescheduling window
- Menu customization
- Service duration (extensions)
- Additional baristas

**Usually Non-Negotiable:**
- Base service rates (market-driven)
- Insurance requirements (legal necessity)
- Minimum service duration (often 2 hours)
- Setup requirements (power, space - cart needs)
- Food safety compliance (legal requirement)

### What to Do If There's No Contract (200 words)

**Request Written Confirmation:**
- Email summary of agreement
- Include: Date, time, services, price, cancellation terms
- Vendor replies confirming = binding agreement

**Key Email Terms to Confirm:**
- "Confirmed: Coffee cart hire for [date] at [venue]"
- Services: X baristas, Y hours, Z menu
- Price: $XXX including GST
- Deposit: $XXX due [date], balance $XXX due [date]
- Cancellation: [summarize policy]
- "Reply to confirm these terms"

**If Vendor Refuses Written Terms:**
- Red flag: Suggests unprofessionalism
- Risk: He-said-she-said if dispute
- Recommendation: Find another vendor
