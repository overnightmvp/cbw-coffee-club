## Menu & Offerings

Roaming Roasters' menu is deliberately multicultural, with two distinct service tracks that can run simultaneously or be emphasized based on event type.

**Greek Coffee Service** is authentic and traditional: coffee is ground to powder fineness, combined with water and sugar (if requested—Greeks have preferences from sketo/unsweetened to varigliko/extra sweet) in copper briki pots, brought to boil three times, and served in small cups with grounds settling. The ritual takes 3-4 minutes per serve and can't be rushed—this is coffee as cultural practice, not just beverage.

Greek coffee is offered in traditional styles:
- **Sketo** (no sugar)
- **Metrio** (medium sweet)
- **Gliko** (sweet)
- **Varigliko** (extra sweet)

This menu precision matters to Greek coffee drinkers who grew up with specific preferences and expect vendors to understand the terminology and preparation differences.

**Specialty Espresso Service** runs the standard Melbourne café menu: flat whites, cappuccinos, lattes, long blacks, macchiatos, and piccolos using quality beans from Melbourne roasters. The espresso program is solid, professional, and appeals to younger generations and non-Greek customers who want quality coffee familiar from their local café.

**Multicultural Coffee Options** extend the menu to reflect broader cultural diversity:
- **Turkish coffee** (similar preparation to Greek, slight variation in grind and serving style)
- **Lebanese coffee** (cardamom-spiced, traditional preparation)
- **Vietnamese iced coffee** (strong coffee over condensed milk and ice, popular at summer festivals)

This multicultural range allows Roaming Roasters to serve genuinely diverse community events where multiple cultural groups gather, each wanting coffee that reflects their traditions.

**Add-ons and complements** align with cultural context: Greek sweets like baklava or kataifi from Oakleigh bakeries, traditional biscuits, and contemporary options like protein balls and gluten-free treats for health-conscious customers. At Greek events, coffee might be served with a traditional spoon sweet (preserved fruit in syrup) following Greek hospitality customs.

Equipment includes traditional Greek coffee equipment (multiple copper briki pots, specialized burners) alongside commercial espresso machines and grinders for specialty coffee service. This dual-equipment setup requires more cart space and setup time but enables authentic multicultural service.
