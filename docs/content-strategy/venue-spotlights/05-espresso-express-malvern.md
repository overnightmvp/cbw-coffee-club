Each category has distinct requirements that Espresso Express has systematized through hundreds of events.

For conferences, they excel at morning arrival service (capturing attendees as they register), timed break service (coordinating with event schedules to have coffee ready when breaks start, not 10 minutes later), and afternoon service (when energy dips and quality coffee matters for focus). Corporate functions like strategy days or training sessions benefit from Espresso Express's flexibility—they can adapt service style to your needs, whether that's continuous coffee availability throughout a full-day workshop or scheduled morning and afternoon service for a half-day executive meeting.

## Menu & Offerings

Espresso Express's menu is strategically simple, engineered for speed without limiting options. The core offering includes all standard espresso drinks: flat whites, cappuccinos, lattes, long blacks, espressos, macchiatos, and piccolos. Each is prepared to consistent specifications using quality Melbourne-roasted beans selected for balanced flavor profiles that appeal to diverse corporate palates—not too fruity or exotic, but well above commodity coffee standards.

The signature offering is **batch brew coffee**, which deserves detailed explanation. Batch brew uses commercial filter coffee equipment to produce larger volumes of quality filter coffee that can be poured in seconds rather than the 30-45 seconds required for espresso drinks. For conferences expecting 300+ attendees to take coffee during a 15-minute break, batch brew is strategic: those wanting simple black coffee can be served in under 10 seconds, dramatically reducing queue times while espresso drinkers are served in parallel.

This dual-service model—espresso machine for milk-based drinks, batch brew for black coffee enthusiasts—is what allows Espresso Express to claim 80-200 guests served per hour. In practice, a well-executed corporate morning break might see 60% of attendees ordering espresso drinks (served at ~80/hour) and 40% ordering batch brew or tea (served at ~200/hour), resulting in blended throughput that keeps lines moving.

Espresso shots are standardized using commercial recipes refined over thousands of corporate events: consistent dose, extraction time, and temperature. This isn't artisanal slow-coffee; it's reliable, quality espresso prepared to repeatable specifications. Milk is steamed efficiently using larger pitchers for back-to-back drinks, maintaining temperature and texture standards but prioritizing speed over latte art.

Add-ons cater to corporate preferences: protein balls and muesli bars (healthier alternatives that align with corporate wellness programs), standard biscotti, and individually-wrapped gluten-free options. Dairy alternatives (oat, almond, soy milk) are standard inclusions, not premium add-ons—corporate events frequently have dietary requirements and inclusive service without surcharges prevents awkward conversations at the coffee station.

Equipment is commercial-grade and redundant: a high-capacity dual-group espresso machine allows two baristas to work simultaneously, commercial batch brew equipment that maintains optimal serving temperature for hours, backup grinders in case of mechanical issues, and portable generators for venues with limited power access. This equipment redundancy matters for corporate events where service failure isn't an option.

## Pricing & Capacity

Espresso Express operates on an hourly booking model with pricing of $160-220 AUD per hour and a three-hour minimum for corporate events. The three-hour minimum is non-negotiable and reflects the reality of corporate service: travel time, equipment setup, calibration, minimum-viable service periods for professional delivery, and breakdown. Most conferences and trade shows run longer than three hours anyway, making this minimum a non-issue.

Pricing variation depends on service complexity and staffing requirements. A standard corporate morning tea at $160/hour includes one barista, standard espresso service, and batch brew for black coffee drinkers. Premium configurations at $220/hour might include two baristas for higher volume (150+ guests), extended service hours, custom-branded cups for sponsor activations, or specialized service requirements like mobile cart repositioning throughout a large expo hall.

What's included in base pricing: all equipment, professional baristas, quality Melbourne-roasted beans, milk, dairy alternatives, cups, lids, stirrers, basic cleanup, and professional insurance (required by most corporate venues). Travel fees apply for locations beyond the Malvern core coverage area but are transparently disclosed during quoting—typically $50-100 for outer Melbourne suburbs or regional events.

Capacity claims are conservative and verified through actual event performance: 80-200 guests per hour depending on menu mix. A conference serving primarily espresso drinks trends toward 80-100 guests/hour. Trade shows where attendees grab quick batch brew between exhibitor visits can hit 150-200/hour. Most corporate planners should plan conservatively for 100/hour unless Espresso Express confirms higher capacity based on your specific menu expectations.

The three-hour minimum booking works out economically for most corporate events: a 200-person conference expecting 75% of attendees to order coffee during morning and afternoon breaks (150 total serves) needs roughly 2-3 hours of actual service. The three-hour booking allows flexibility for extended breaks, executive team second cups, or networking periods that run longer than scheduled.

For full-day conferences or multi-day events, Espresso Express offers daily rates that provide better value than adding hourly increments. A typical full-day conference package (7:30 AM arrival service through 4:00 PM afternoon break) runs $800-1,200 depending on expected volume and staffing requirements—more economical than 8+ hourly bookings.
