## Menu & Offerings

Mobile Brew's menu is deliberately focused to maximize speed without limiting options. The core espresso offerings cover what 90% of guests want: flat whites, cappuccinos, lattes, long blacks, espressos, and macchiatos. Each drink is prepared to specialty coffee standards using quality beans from Melbourne roasters, but the workflow is optimized—grind settings are dialed in before service, milk is steamed in larger batches for back-to-back drinks, and baristas follow standardized recipes that ensure consistency at speed.

Cold brew is Mobile Brew's signature offering, especially popular at summer festivals and outdoor corporate events. Their proprietary cold brew blend is brewed in large batches using a 16-hour extraction process, then served over ice with options for milk, simple syrup, or vanilla. It's refreshing, lower in acidity than hot coffee, and critically, can be poured in seconds—perfect for long festival queues.

Specialty drinks are kept minimal but strategic: iced lattes for warm-weather events, hot chocolate for family gatherings and younger attendees, and a simple chai latte option. This focused menu isn't about lacking variety; it's about mastering execution. When a barista can make six drinks they've perfected rather than 20 they're learning, quality stays consistent even during peak rushes.

Equipment drives their efficiency promise. The dual-group La Marzocco espresso machine maintains precise temperature control crucial for proper extraction, while high-capacity grinders can handle continuous use without overheating. The cold brew kegs are kept at optimal serving temperature in built-in refrigeration, meaning no waiting for chilling or dilution from excessive ice.

Add-ons are practical rather than gourmet: individually wrapped biscotti, protein balls from local suppliers, and complimentary dairy alternatives (oat, almond, soy milk). They understand that corporate wellness programs and dietary restrictions are standard considerations, so inclusive options are built into base pricing.

## Pricing & Capacity

Mobile Brew operates on an hourly rate of $150-200 AUD per hour with a two-hour minimum booking. This pricing positions them competitively for budget-conscious corporate clients and community organizations while reflecting the quality of their service and beans. The lower end ($150/hour) applies to standard corporate morning teas or afternoon breaks; the higher rate ($200/hour) covers festivals or complex events requiring extended setup, additional staff, or specialized cold brew service.

What's included in the base rate deserves emphasis: setup and breakdown, all equipment, coffee beans, milk, dairy alternatives, cups, lids, and basic cleanup. There are no surprise add-on fees for oat milk or takeaway cups—Mobile Brew builds these essentials into transparent pricing. Additional costs only apply for travel beyond their core Fountain Gate coverage area (typically $50-75 for outer suburbs) or premium add-ons like branded cups or custom menu boards.

Capacity is where Mobile Brew truly delivers value. They guarantee 60-180 guests served per hour depending on drink complexity. A corporate event serving primarily simple espressos and long blacks can hit the upper range; a festival crowd ordering complex iced drinks trends toward the lower end. For most events, they recommend planning for 100 guests per hour as a conservative estimate that ensures no one waits more than a few minutes.

The two-hour minimum booking makes sense for their service model—it accounts for travel time, equipment setup, warming the espresso machine to proper temperature, and allowing baristas to hit their rhythm. For events expecting 150-200 total guests, two hours provides comfortable service without rushing. Larger corporate conferences or all-day festivals can book extended hours, with pricing typically discounted slightly for full-day commitments.
